üîç Feature 1: Holes (Removable Discontinuities)

What is a hole?

A hole occurs when a factor cancels from both numerator and denominator.

Example with a hole: \(f(x) = \frac{(x-2)(x+1)}{(x-2)(x+3)} = \frac{x+1}{x+3}\) has a hole at \(x = 2\)

Check our function:

Denominator: \(2x + 4 = 2(x + 2)\)

‚úì No common factors between numerator and denominator

Holes: NONE - No common factors to cancel

üîç Feature 2: Slant (Oblique) Asymptote

When does a slant asymptote occur?

When degree of numerator = degree of denominator + 1

Example: \(f(x) = \frac{x^2 + 1}{x - 2}\) has degree 2 in numerator, degree 1 in denominator

Use polynomial long division to find the slant asymptote equation

Check our function:

Denominator degree: 1

Condition needed: 1 = 1 + 1? ‚úó FALSE

Slant Asymptote: NONE - Degrees are equal (horizontal asymptote instead)
